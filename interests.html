Interests

My Interests:


Sport

  • Sport is an important part of my life. I enjoy most of my time being outdoors during the summer. There is many benefits from playing sports such as improving physical and mental health, boosting self-confidence and learning time management and discipline.

  • I love many types of sport. The sports I play are soccer, GAA, basketball, surfing and skateboarding. My favourite sport is soccer. I play for my local soccer team and I joined the UCC ladies soccer team this year. I play midfield for my local team and then striker for UCC. This year the UCC soccer team are doing very well and we are in a huge competition in the end of March called intervarsities, where we play against the best colleges in Ireland.

  • I also love extreme sports such as skateboarding and surfing. These sports are very exhilarating and skillful. I only learnt how to skateboard 3 years ago when I found my brother's skateboard and I've learnt many tricks ever since. I am also very happy that at this years 2020 Summer Olympics, skateboarding is an event. Besides skateboarding, during the summer I love to surf. I learnt how to surf after learning how to skateboard as my balance improved. Even though the water can be cold during the summer it is all worthwhile, when you get up on the board.I love watching videos on YouTube of these extreme sports as some people can do crazy skills and I admire them.



Travel

  • I have always been interested in travelling the world. When I was younger I made a list of all the places I wanted to travel to. Although I haven't got the chance to visit most of the countries so far, I will definitely make time now that I am in college.

  • There are many benefits of travelling such as discovering the world's incredible culture, having incredible adventures, you might learn a new language and it also puts life in perspective.

  • Last summer I visited London. The sites were incredible. I visited the Big Ben, The London Eye and Buckingham Palace. I walked through Hyde Park and it was absolutely beautiful. We were lucky enough with the good weather. My family and I also got the chance to visit Wimbledon Stadium, but unfortunately we didn't have time to see a tennis match. The biggest highlight fo me was visiting Stamford Bridge as I am a big Chelsea FC fan. We got a tour of the stadium and the dressing rooms which was great.

  • Two years ago, it was my first time on a plane. My family and I visited Spain. We went to Torremolinos in the south of Spain. This was very beneficial to me as I was studying Spanish for the Leaving Certificate. I learnt a lot from speaking spanish to local people and reading the spanish newspaper. The weather was great and I spent most of my time at the beach.
