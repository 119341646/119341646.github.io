Hyperautomation


Hyperautomation brings together technologies, integrating tools and components of process automation. Hyperautomation uses technology to automate tasks that once required humans. Hyperautomation deals with the application of advanced technologies, including artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML),to increasingly automate processes and augment humans. Hyperautomation uses the correct technologies and extends a range of tools that can be automated, but at the same time refers to the sophistication of the automation like measure, automate, analyse, monitor and design. In recent times, Hyperautomation often results in the creation of a digital twin of the organization.Hyperautomation involves a combination of tools, including robotic process automation (RPA), intelligent business management software (iBPMS) and AI, with a goal of increasingly AI-driven decision making. Hyperautomation uses a combination of tools because no single tool can replace humans.

Multiexperience


Application development is going multiexperience to evolve with the way people are now engaging with businesses, individuals, and systems.These changes are reinventing the way applications are being built across the digital landscape to accommodate interactions via touch, voice, and gesture. Multiexperience is a development platform that is used for developing not just mobile and web applications, but also chat, voice, augmented reality and wearable experiences.Development platforms have removed the boundary between web and mobile development efforts to meet customer expectations of building across digital touchpoints. A multi-experience development platform (MXDP) are helping businesses create low code user experiences. Marketing technology leaders should assess multiexperience development platforms as a means of developing the best app user experiences.

As user application touchpoints increase in frequency, the future of app development is multiexperience, according to a recent survey by Gartner, Inc. According to a research vice president at Gartner;“Development platform vendors are expanding their value proposition beyond mobile apps and web development to meet user and industry demands,”

Distributed Cloud



Distributed Cloud is the examination of a major evolution in cloud computing where the tools, management, applications, platforms, security, and other services are changing from a centralized data center model to one in which the services are distributed and delivered at the direct point of need. Distributed cloud is the application of cloud computing technologies to interconnect data and applications served from multiple geographic locations. The point of need can extend into customer data centers or all the way to the edge devices. As more tools and innovations become useful for central management, distributed cloud architectures are expected to develop quickly and become the norm for many organizations.

The distributed cloud is the application of cloud computing technologies to connect data and functions which are located in different physical locations.Cloud customers can gather at a given distributed cloud substation to consume cloud services for common or varied reasons if it is open for community or public use. This improves the economics associated with paying for the installation and operation of a distributed cloud substation. As other companies use the substation, they can share the cost of the installation.
